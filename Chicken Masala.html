Chicken Masala

Ingredients

Mushrooms
Marsala wine
Flour
Salt
Pepper
Butter
Chicken broth
Olive oil
Garlic
chicken breast
Heavy whipping cream
Sage

Equipment

Chopping board
Large pan
Pounder

Method

1. If your chicken breasts are large, like the ones in the photo above, it’s best to first cut them in half horizontally.
2. Once you’ve got four flat filets, pound them each
3. Place the flour, 3/4 teaspoon salt, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper in a ziplock bag.
4. Add the chicken to the bag; seal the bag tightly and shake to coat chicken evenly. Set aside.
5. Heat the oil and 2 tablespoons of the butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
(Use a stainless steel pan for the best browning. Nonstick will work too, but you won’t get that nice golden color on the chicken.) Place the flour-dusted chicken in the pan, shaking off any excess first.
6. Cook, turning once, until the chicken is golden and just barely cooked through, about 5 to 6 minutes total. Transfer the chicken to a plate and set aside.
7. Melt the remaining tablespoon of butter in the pan. Add the mushrooms and cook, stirring frequently, until the mushrooms begin to brown, 3 to 4 minutes.
8. Add the shallots, garlic, and ¼ teaspoon of salt and cook for 1 to 2 minutes more.
9. Add the broth, wine, heavy cream, thyme, 1/4 teaspoon salt, and 1/8 teaspoon of pepper; use a wooden spoon to scrape any brown bits from the pan into the liquid. Bring the liquid to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium.
10. Gently boil, uncovered, until the sauce is reduced by about half, slightly thickened, and darkened in color, 10 to 15 minutes (you’re going for a thin cream sauce; it won’t start to thicken until the very end of the cooking time).
11. Add the chicken back to the pan, along with any juices that accumulated on the plate. Reduce the heat to low and simmer until the chicken is warmed through and the sauce thickens a bit more, 2 to 3 minutes.
12. Sprinkle with parsley, if using, and serve.
